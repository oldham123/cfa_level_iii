# Reading 6: Professionalism in Investment Management

## 6.A: Describe professions and how they establish trust

- A **profession** is:
  - An occupational group with specific education and expert knowledge
    - Found within the CFA curriculum
  - With a framework of practice and behaviour
    - Found within *The Standards of Practice Handbook*
  - That emphasise an ethical approach, and the importance of good service
    - Reflected in the importance of ethics within the curriculum

- Professions can establish trust in multiple ways:
  - Normalising their behaviour though codes and standards
  - Providing service that exceeds those standards
  - Maintaining a focus on client interests
  - High standards for entry
  - Possession of a body of expert knowledge
  - Encouraging continuing education
  - Monitoring conduct
  - Collegiality
  - Establishing recognised oversight bodies
  - Encouraging volunteerism

## 6.B: Explain professionalism in investment management

- Investment management is a relatively new profession
  - The understanding of, and framework behind, the profession are still developing

- Trust is earned by demonstrating:
  - Technical expertise
  - Knowledge of applicable law
  - An understanding of ethics

- The CFA Institute is a body that represents many of the tenets of a profession:
  - The mission of the CFAI is "to lead the investment profession globally, by promoting the highest standards of ethics, education, and professional excellence for the ultimate benefit of society"
  - The CFAI maintains the *Global Body* and *Candidate Body of Investment Knowledge*, articulating the competencies necessary
  - The CFAI engages members around the world with volunteer and education oppotunities
  - Charterholders and Candidates must certify every year that they adhere to the Code and Standards

## 6.C: Describe expectations of and challenges for investment management professionals

- Expectations include:
  - Act with integrity, competence, diligence, respect, and in an ethical manner with the public, clients, prospective clients, employers, employees, colleagues, and other participants in the global capital markets
  - PLace the integrity of the profession and the interests of clients above personal interests
  - USe reasonable care and independent professional judgement in analysis, recommendations, actions, and other professional activity
  - Practice and encourage others to practice in a professional and ethical manner reflecting credit on themselves and the profession
  - Promote the integrity and viability of the global capital markets for the ultimate benefit of society
  - Maintain and improve professional competence and strive to maintain and improve the competence of other investment professionals

- Challenges include:
  - The recent erosion of respect towards experts in general, and investment professionals in particular
  - Consumerism means investment products are purchased like many other items, increasing the demands on the profession
  - Regulations are now stricter
  - Globalisation presents a greater variety of customers with more unique needs, demands, and expectations
  - Technological innovation is rapidly changing the role of the investment professional and their relationship with the client
