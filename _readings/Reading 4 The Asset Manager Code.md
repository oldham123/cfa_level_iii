# Reading 4: The Asset Manager Code

## 4.A: Explain the purpose of the Asset Manager Code and the benefits that may accrue to a firm that adopts the Code

- The Code is global and voluntary, applying to investment management firms
  - Encouraged as a template and guidepost
  - Demonstrates that client interests are first
  - Flexible, and requires firm-specific tailoring

## 4.D: Recommend practices and procedures designed to prevent violations of the AMC

- The six components of the AMC are:
  - **Loyalty to clients**
    - Align compensation to avoid conflict with client interests
    - Create a privacy policy documenting how information should be gathered, stored, and used
    - Create and anti-money laundering policy
    - Refuse gifts and entertainment of more than token value
    - Define gift limits, and require disclosure of gifts
    - Prohibit cash gifts
  - **Investment process and actions**
    - Use reasonable care and judgement in management of client assets
    - Do not manipulate prices or volumes with intent to mislead
    - Deal fairly with all clients when providing information, advice, or taking actions
    - Higher service levels are acceptable if disclosed and available to anyone who will pay for them
    - Secondary investment opportunities are acceptable if fairly allocated to all suitable clients
    - There should be a reasonable and adequate basis for recommendations
    - Managers do not have to evaluate suitability for portfolios managed to a specific style or strategy
    - There must be adequate disclosure so that clients can evaluate whether specific portfolios are right for them
    - Flexibility and deviations in style must be communicated to clients
  - **Trading**
    - Do not act on, or cause others to act on material nonpublic information
    - Make use of firewalls to prevent dissemination of nonpublic information
    - Develop procedues to evaluate whether information is material or nonpublic
    - Give clients priority over the firm
    - Personal capital can be invested with client capital provided clients are not disadvantaged
    - Monitor and limit personal trading by employees where necessary
    - Establish watch lists of companies in which personal investment requires approval
    - Client commissions should only be used to benefit the client
    - If clients direct trading, advise clients it may compromise the ability to seek best execution, seeking written acknolwedgement of this fact
    - Group suitable accounts and trade as a block, allocating partial trades pro-rata across the block
  - **Risk management, compliance, and support**
    - Compliance officers should be independent of investment and operational personnel
    - Use an independent third party to verify that information given to clients is accurate and complete
    - Verification should not just use internal records
    - Retain compliance records and documentation of violations and corrective actions
    - Retain records for at least seven years
    - Establish business continuity plans to deal with disasters or market disruptions
    - Implement backups of records, preferably offsite
    - Make plans for employee and client communication in the event that normal communications fail
  - **Performance and valuation**
    - Adopt GIPS
  - **Disclosures**
    - Disclosures should be ongoing and timely
    - Disclosures should be truthful, accurate, clear, and complete
    - Disclosures should include:
      - Conflicts of interest
      - Regulatory and disciplinary actions
      - Information regarding the investment process
      - Management fees, client costs, the method of calculation
      - Gross and net of fee returns
      - Unusual expenses
      - Soft dollar and bundled fees
      - Quarterly performance within 30 days of the quarter ending
      - Valuation methods
      - Policies for shareholder voting
      - Trade allocation policies
      - Signifiant personnel changes
- The six general principles of conduct in the AMC are:
  - Act ethically and professionally
  - Act in the best interests of the client
  - Act in an objective and independent manner
  - Perform with skill, competence, and diligence
  - Comply with legal and regulatory requirements
